## Design Context

### Users
- Primary users are developers and technical operators who keep AI agent activity visible while they work.
- The interface sits in the MacBook notch area as a high-frequency ambient monitor, not a dashboard people study for minutes at a time.
- The core job is fast situational awareness: who is active, what they are doing, whether attention is needed, and whether the island is worth expanding.

### Brand Personality
- System-grade
- Restrained
- Quietly premium
- The interface should evoke confidence, calm, and precision rather than excitement or novelty.

### Aesthetic Direction
- The visual direction is macOS-adjacent but not a direct imitation of first-party Apple UI.
- The product should feel like a mature system utility with refined material depth, strong information hierarchy, and low-noise motion.
- Dark presentation is the default mode.
- Keep Claude and Codex identity cues, but use them as restrained recognition accents instead of sweet or playful candy colors.
- Avoid AI-slop patterns such as purple-blue gradients, decorative glassmorphism, oversized glows, noisy chrome, and over-animated mascots.

### Design Principles
- Information earns visual weight: task and state first, metadata second, diagnostics last.
- Material coherence over effect density: shell, card, border, glow, and shadow must feel like one optical system.
- Quiet motion beats expressive motion: transitions should feel inevitable, not attention-seeking.
- Typography should separate narrative content from instrument data.
- Premium comes from restraint, alignment, and hierarchy, not ornament.
